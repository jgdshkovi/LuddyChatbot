Why Kelley is better
Unmatched professional development experiences
Before your MBA classes even begin, you’ll bond with your new classmates in Me, Inc., our one-of-a-kind business professional development workshop. You’ll experience intensive career development along with team-building activities to prepare you for the next two years at Kelley—and your career. It’s the start of unwavering career support from a team committed to your success.
Explore career support services
Choose MBA majors and minors that further your career goals
You’ll combine our challenging integrated core curriculum, which strengthens your business acumen in key areas such as finance, marketing, quantitative analysis, and strategy, with majors and minors that focus on your interests. Five of these seven majors are STEM designated.
With our smaller class sizes of 125 to 150 students, you'll get personalized attention from faculty, your career coach, and from your talented peers. We all work with you as you prepare for recruiting and choose from the MBA majors, minors, and certificates that can help you achieve your career goals. Students who +Flex their MBA second year online will learn from the same professors who teach in-person classes.

Choosing an MBA is a big decision—let's talk about it together
We offer many ways for you to meet us.
Join a virtual roundtable discussion or attend virtual office hours hosted by members of the admissions team. During roundtable discussions you’ll join a group of prospective students for a virtual information session and discussion led by members of the admissions team and student ambassadors. We hold these discussions bi-weekly when classes are in session. Virtual office hours give you the opportunity to drop in for quick answers from Kelley MBA admissions staff.
*Note about visa considerations:
For international students to maintain F-1 visa status, you must enroll in a full load of in-person classes each fall and spring semester. For graduate students, that is at least 8 credit hours per semester.
